Abstract

As Large Language Models (LLMs) are widely used in reasoning and planning, they have shown promising capabilities in robotics. However, LLMs have also shown weaknesses in complex logical reasoning and sometimes "hallucinate" false answers with high confidence. To address these problems, in this paper, we present a framework to apply LLMs as robot task planners with mission requirements specified as Linear Temporal Logic (LTL) . Meanwhile, we provide a toolbox to quantify the uncertainty of language model responses using conformal prediction. Extensive experiments with different levels of task complexity show that our framework performs favorably over the current baseline in terms of complex logical requirement fulfillment while providing statistical analysis of the task completeness.
